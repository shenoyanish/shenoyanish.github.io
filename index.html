Anish Shenoy

I am a Data Scientist at Intel, where I develop computational algorithms to enable successful lithographic patterning of Intel's microprocessors.

I got my M.S. and Ph.D. degrees at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where I was advised by Charles M. Schroeder and Sascha Hilgenfeldt. Previously, I completed my bachelor's degree at Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, where I worked with Suman Chakraborty.

I'm broadly interested in the fields of computer vision, robotics, and autonomous driving. To this end, I have completed extensive coursework in machine learning, statistics, computer vision and deep learning at the University of Illinois. During my PhD, I developed a new technology, called the Stokes Trap, that combined image processing and nonlinear optimal control to precisely manipulate microscopic particles using fluid flow. My research has been covered on Engineering at Illinois (Orientation Control, Stokes Trap) and Nature Physics, and has won best paper/presentation awards at national ACS/AIChE conferences.

Projects
Behavioural cloning
Self-driving Car Engineer Nanodegree Program, Udacity

Trained a neural network to drive a car in a simulated race track by cloning a human driver's input. Collected training data by manually driving a car around race track several times and recording the front-view, left-view and right-view for each instant as well as the steering angles. Used the NVIDIA end to end model to map images to the steering wheel angles

Advanced lane lines detection
Self-driving Car Engineer Nanodegree Program, Udacity

Used computer vision techniques to identify curved lane lines and the driveable portion of the road. Detected the curvature of the road using the lane lines and also detected the offset of the vehicle from the middle of the road. Implemented methods to improve the robustness of lane detection in the presence of surface irregularities and shadows
Neural style transfer using convolutional neural networks
Course Project, ECE 543 Computer Vision, Spring 2017, UIUC
Instructor: Prof. Derek Hoeim

Implemented Neural Style Transfer as described in the paper by Gatys et al. (2016). Studied the effect of weights on the final rendered image as well as transferring style while preserving color
